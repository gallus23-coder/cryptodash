Indicator Glossary
RSI-14 (Relative Strength Index)
Measures the speed and magnitude of price changes on a 0–100 scale. Below 30 = oversold (potential buy), above 70 = overbought (potential sell). Calculated over 14 candles using Wilder's smoothing.
MACD (12/26/9)
Moving Average Convergence Divergence. The MACD line is EMA12 minus EMA26. Signal line is EMA9 of MACD. Histogram = MACD − Signal. MACD above zero and rising histogram = bullish momentum.
Bollinger Bands (20, ±2σ)
Middle band = 20-period SMA. Upper/lower bands = ±2 standard deviations. Price near lower band = potentially oversold. Bandwidth% shows volatility: narrow = squeeze, wide = expansion.
EMA50 / EMA200
Exponential Moving Averages over 50 and 200 candles. Price above EMA200 = long-term uptrend. EMA50 crossing above EMA200 = golden cross (bullish). EMA50 below EMA200 = death cross (bearish).
Stochastic RSI (14/14/3/3)
Applies a stochastic oscillator to RSI values. %K below 20 = oversold, above 80 = overbought. %D is a 3-period SMA of %K. More sensitive than RSI alone.
Volume Ratio
Current candle volume divided by the 20-period average. >1.5 = elevated volume confirming the move. <0.7 = low conviction. High volume on up candles = accumulation.
Fear & Greed Index
Alternative.me composite index (0–100). 0–24 = Extreme Fear, 25–49 = Fear, 50–74 = Greed, 75–100 = Extreme Greed. Contrarian indicator: extreme fear often precedes recoveries.
